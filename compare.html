The meta-question: which one should you pick?

If you run an online-first US business with ~2,000+ transactions/month: Stripe. Developer ergonomics, international flexibility, and Connect platform tooling dominate at scale.

If you're a small retailer, restaurant, or service business doing in-person sales: Square. The 2.6% + $0.10 in-person rate, free base plan, and no-chargeback-fees policy compound to real savings.

If you sell primarily to buyers who prefer PayPal's Checkout experience (older demographics, international hobbyist markets, niche categories): PayPal, despite higher rates. Buyer trust lifts conversion; lift > fee delta in those segments.

If you run a B2B invoicing business with large tickets: Square ACH at 1% (min $1) on invoice-paid-via-bank is often the single cheapest option available.

Most real businesses end up using two processors: one primary, one backup. Stripe + Square is a common pairing. Stripe + PayPal is another. Decide based on the math — that's what this site exists for.

Frequently asked questions

Why is Stripe's in-person rate Terminal-only?
Stripe doesn't offer a standalone Reader like Square. In-person Stripe requires Stripe Terminal (hardware SDK for point-of-sale apps) or a partner integration. For a small business that just wants a tap-to-pay device, Square is dramatically easier to set up.
How do I actually run the numbers for my business?
Take your last month's transactions. Bucket them by type (online domestic, in-person, international, invoice). Multiply each bucket by each processor's rate. The one with the lowest total wins. Our Stripe, Square, and PayPal calculators make the per-bucket math easy.
Does PayPal's brand trust really outweigh its higher fees?
Sometimes. Studies on PayPal conversion lift vs. card-only checkout generally show 10–30% conversion improvements in certain audiences. At 3.49% + $0.49 vs 2.9% + $0.30, PayPal costs about 0.6% more per transaction. If PayPal lifts your conversion by more than 0.6%, it pays for itself. Test it with your own audience.
What about Shopify Payments, Braintree, Adyen?
Shopify Payments (Stripe under the hood, priced at 2.9% + $0.30 online for Basic plan, lower for Plus) is compelling if you're already on Shopify. Braintree (owned by PayPal, priced like Stripe) is the enterprise option. Adyen is strong for international at scale. We'll add calculators for these — fastest-follow is Shopify.
